1.2.2 Find the equations of \vec{AD},\vec{BE},\vec{CF} where \vec{D},\vec{E},\vec{F} are midpoints of the triangle \vec{ABC}. The vertices \vec{A,B,C} are:
\begin{align}
	\vec {A}= &\myvec{1\\-1};\\
	\vec {B}= &\myvec{-4\\6};\\
	\vec {C}= &\myvec{-3\\-5};
\end{align}
\\ \solution

Given this information and using the midpoint values found in the questions 1.2.1, the midpoints D,E,F are:

\begin{align}
	\vec {D}= &\myvec{\frac{-7}{2}\\\frac{1}{2}};\\
	\vec {E}= &\myvec{-1\\-3};\\
	\vec {F}= &\myvec{\frac{-3}{2}\\\frac{5}{2}};
\end{align}

The normal equation for the side $AD$ is
\begin{align}
\vec{n}^{\top}\myvec{\vec{x}-\vec{A}}&=0\\
\implies
\vec{n}^{\top}\vec{x}&=\vec{n}^{\top}\vec{A}
\end{align}
Now we should find $\vec{n}$ so that we can find $\vec{n}^{\top}$.

\begin{align}
  \vec{n} &= \myvec{0 & 1\\-1 & 0}\vec{m}
\end{align}
Where $\vec{m} = \vec{D}- \vec{A}$ for median $\vec{AD}$
\begin{align}
\implies
\vec{m}&=\myvec{\frac{-7}{2}\\\frac{1}{2}} - \myvec{1\\-1}\\
&=\myvec{\frac{-9}{2}\\\frac{3}{2}}
\end{align}

we can use this to obtain vector $\vec{n}$
\begin{align}
\vec{n} &= \myvec{0 & 1\\
  -1 & 0}\myvec{\frac{-9}{2}\\\frac{3}{2}}
 = \myvec{\frac{3}{2}\\\frac{9}{2}}
\end{align}
The transpose of $\vec{n}$ is
\begin{align}
  \vec{n}^{\top}&=\myvec{\frac{3}{2} & \frac{9}{2}}
\end{align}
Hence the normal equation of side $AD$ is 
\begin{align}
    \myvec{\frac{3}{2} & \frac{9}{2}}\vec{x}&=\myvec{\frac{3}{2} & \frac{9}{2}}\myvec{1\\-1}\\
    \myvec{\frac{3}{2} & \frac{9}{2}}\vec{x}&=-3
\end{align}


The normal equation for the side $BE$ is
\begin{align}
\vec{n}^{\top}\myvec{\vec{x}-\vec{B}}&=0\\
\vec{n}^{\top}\vec{x}&=\vec{n}^{\top}\vec{B}
\end{align}
Now we should find $\vec{n}$ so that we can find $\vec{n}^{\top}$.

\begin{align}
  \vec{n} &= \myvec{0 & 1\\-1 & 0}\vec{m}
\end{align}
Where $\vec{m} = \vec{E}- \vec{B}$ for median $\vec{BE}$
\begin{align}
\vec{m}&=\myvec{-1\\-3} - \myvec{-4\\6}\\
&=\myvec{3\\-9}
\end{align}

we can use this to obtain vector $\vec{n}$
\begin{align}
\vec{n} &= \myvec{0 & 1\\
  -1 & 0}\myvec{3\\-9}
 = \myvec{-9\\-3}
\end{align}
The transpose of $\vec{n}$ is
\begin{align}
  \vec{n}^{\top}&=\myvec{-9 & -3}
\end{align}
Hence the normal equation of side $BE$ is 
\begin{align}
    \myvec{-9 & -3}\vec{x}&=\myvec{-9 & -3}\myvec{-4\\6}\\
    \implies
    \myvec{-9 & -3}\vec{x}&=18
\end{align}



The normal equation for the side $CF$ is
\begin{align}
\vec{n}^{\top}\myvec{\vec{x}-\vec{C}}&=0\\
\implies
\vec{n}^{\top}\vec{x}&=\vec{n}^{\top}\vec{C}
\end{align}
Now we should find $\vec{n}$ so that we can find $\vec{n}^{\top}$.

\begin{align}
  \vec{n} &= \myvec{0 & 1\\-1 & 0}\vec{m}
\end{align}
Where $\vec{m} = \vec{F}- \vec{C}$ for median $\vec{CF}$
\begin{align}
\implies
\vec{m}&=\myvec{\frac{-3}{2}\\\frac{5}{2}} - \myvec{-3\\-5}\\
&=\myvec{\frac{3}{2}\\\frac{15}{2}}
\end{align}

we can use this to obtain vector $\vec{n}$
\begin{align}
\vec{n} &= \myvec{0 & 1\\
  -1 & 0}\myvec{\frac{3}{2}\\\frac{15}{2}}
 = \myvec{\frac{15}{2}\\\frac{-3}{2}}
\end{align}
The transpose of $\vec{n}$ is
\begin{align}
  \vec{n}^{\top}&=\myvec{\frac{15}{2} & \frac{-3}{2}}
\end{align}
Hence the normal equation of side $CF$ is 
\begin{align}
    \myvec{\frac{15}{2} & \frac{-3}{2}}\vec{x}&=\myvec{\frac{15}{2} & \frac{-3}{2}}\myvec{-3\\-5}\\
    \implies
   \myvec{\frac{15}{2} & \frac{-3}{2}}\vec{x}&=-15
\end{align}

The equations of the medians AD,BE,CF are:

\begin{enumerate}
 \item $AD$: \myvec{\frac{3}{2} & \frac{9}{2}}\vec{x}&=-3
 \item $BE$: \myvec{-9 & -3}\vec{x}&=18
 \item $CF$: \myvec{\frac{15}{2} & \frac{-3}{2}}\vec{x}&=-15
\end{enumerate}
